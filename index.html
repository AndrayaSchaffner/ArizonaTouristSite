Officially Arizona
  • The Saguaro cactus blossom is the official state flower. The white flower blooms on the tips of the Saguaro cactus during May & June.
  • Petrified wood is the official state fossil. Most petrified wood comes from the Petrified Forest in northeastern Arizona.
  • The Palo verde is the official state tree. It's name means green stick & it blooms a brilliant yellow-gold in April-May.
  • The cactus wren is the official state bird. It grows 7-8 inches long & likes to build nests in the protection of thorny desert plants like the arms of the Saguaro cactus.
  • Turquoise is the official state gemstone. The blue-green stone has a waxy surface & can be found throughout the state.
  • The ringtail is the official state mammal. The ringtail is a small fox-like animal about 2 1/2 foot long & is a shy nocturnal creature.
  • The Arizona tree frog is the official amphibian. The frog is actually between 3/4 & 2 inches long.
